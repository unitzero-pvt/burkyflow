No consistent qualification process across reps
Warm leads going cold due to delayed or missed follow-up
CRM records missing key seller information
No automated notification system when a lead booked in

THE SOLUTION
Mark — an AI outbound follow-up specialist built on VAPI
Pinnacle deployed "Mark," a conversational AI outbound voice agent built on VAPI, to handle all follow-up calls to warm leads. Mark's sole job on every call is to collect four qualification data points and, if at least two are met, book an appointment directly into GoHighLevel all without human involvement.
Mark is designed to sound like a real follow-up specialist: short sentences, no robotic filler phrases, one question at a time so homeowners engage naturally without realising they are speaking to an AI.

HOW IT WORKS — STEP BY STEP

1
Outbound call initiated
Mark dials the homeowner from Pinnacle's existing lead list. He opens with a warm, familiar tone — acknowledging prior contact — and asks one direct question: are they still thinking about selling?


2
Pillar qualification — one question at a time
Mark works through timeline, motivation, condition, and price in a natural conversational order. Every response is under three sentences. There are no scripted confirmations, no repeated phrases, and no hard sell. If the homeowner's answer to one question covers a second pillar, Mark adapts and moves forward.


3
Real-time calendar availability check
When 2 or more pillars pass, Mark calls the GoHighLevel calendar availability tool live during the call. He pauses naturally — saying he'll check what's open — retrieves available slots, and offers up to three options within Monday to Friday, 9am to 6pm.


4
Appointment booked in GoHighLevel
Once the homeowner confirms a time, Mark stores the appointment date, time, name, and phone number as structured outputs. The appointment is created directly in the GoHighLevel calendar with no human step required.


5
Contact lookup and CRM update
After the call ends, the backend searches GoHighLevel for the contact by phone number. If the contact exists, their record is updated. If not, a new contact is created. All four pillar responses are written into custom fields in GHL automatically.


6
Owner and staff notifications
As soon as a call ends with a booked appointment, the Pinnacle team receives an automated email notification and an SMS alert containing the lead's name, phone number, appointment time, and a summary of their four pillar answers.


7
Lead confirmation SMS
The homeowner receives an automated SMS confirming their appointment — including the day, time, and who will be calling them sent directly to the number they called from.

KEY DESIGN DECISIONS
Mark is intentionally concise. Every design choice — one question at a time, responses under three sentences, no enthusiasm fillers, no re-pitching on objections — mirrors how an experienced human follow-up specialist actually speaks. The result is a caller experience that feels natural rather than automated, which improves engagement and the quality of answers collected.
The backend was built around the reality of real estate lead lists: contacts may or may not already exist in GoHighLevel, and phone numbers are the most reliable identifier. The phone-first lookup-then-create logic ensures no duplicate contacts are ever created and no call outcome is lost.

HOW THE AI RECEPTIONIST WORKS — STEP BY STEP

1
Spam gate check
Every incoming call is first checked against the spam number database before the agent even answers. If the number matches any of the 40,000+ logged entries, the call is rejected immediately and never reaches the agent. Zero cost, zero agent time used.


2
Warm inbound greeting
For calls that pass the spam gate, the agent answers with a friendly, professional greeting specific to the service brand. It does not immediately push into booking — it listens first and responds to what the caller actually needs.


3
Service and pricing questions
If the caller is asking about services or pricing, the agent answers accurately from the configured service list. Pricing is only shared when the caller asks. The agent never volunteers pricing unprompted and always clarifies that exact costs are confirmed by the technician on-site.


4
Scheduling intent detection
The agent only moves into booking when the caller clearly signals they want to schedule. It does not ask about dates or availability unless the caller brings it up. This prevents premature booking flows and keeps the conversation natural.


5
Information collection in strict order
When booking intent is confirmed, the agent collects all required information one question at a time: full name, phone number, service address, zip code, preferred date, and preferred 3-hour time window. No step is skipped.


6
Scheduling rules enforcement
Before confirming any date, the agent checks: Is it a Saturday? (Always closed.) Is it same-day Friday? (Not allowed — Sunday offered.) Is the date on the blackout list? (Fully booked — nearest available suggested.) All other dates are open and booked normally.


7
Appointment confirmed and booked
Once all information is collected and the date passes all checks, the appointment is booked. The agent confirms the day and date back to the caller — never the year — and thanks them before closing the call.


8
Team notification and lead SMS
As soon as an appointment is booked, the owner and relevant staff receive an automated email and SMS with the caller's name, phone, address, zip code, service type, and confirmed appointment window. The caller receives a separate SMS confirming their booking.


9
Spam detection and logging
If during any call the agent determines the caller is spam, it ends the call politely and immediately logs the number to the spam database. That number is permanently blocked from all future calls.



THE SPAM BLOCKING SYSTEM — IN DETAIL
This system was built entirely from scratch to solve a problem that was genuinely costing the client money on every spam call received. Before it existed, the client was receiving over 1,000 spam calls regularly — each one connecting to their phone infrastructure, consuming call minutes, and in some cases reaching a live person.
The solution works in three layers:

Layer 1 — Pre-answer number check
Every call is screened against the spam database before it is answered. If the number is on the list, the call is blocked at the infrastructure level. Zero cost, zero agent involvement — the call never connects.
Layer 2 — In-call spam detection
For numbers not yet in the database, the AI agent handles the call normally. If the agent detects spam behaviour during the call — robotic patterns, irrelevant solicitation, known spam scripts — it ends the call and flags the number for blocking.
Layer 3 — Automatic database logging
Every newly detected spam number is written to the central database automatically. No manual review required. The database grows continuously and becomes more accurate and effective over time.
